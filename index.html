GSLM-SS:

Generative Speech Language Model for Speech Separation


Tianhua Li1, Chenda Li1, Wei Wang1, Xin Zhou1, Jianqing Gao2, Yanmin Qian1

1Auditory Cognition and Computational Acoustics Lab
MoE Key Lab of Artificial Intelligence, AI Institute
School of Computer Science, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China
2AI research Institute, iFLYTEK Company Limited, Hefei Anhui, China

Abstract. Speech separation (SS) has advanced significantly with neural network-based methods, improving signal-level performance. However, these methods often struggle with maintaining linguistic consistency in the separated signals, which can affect the performance of downstream tasks such as speech recognition. In this work, we propose a novel approach that applies generative speech language models to SS, aiming to improve the linguistic coherence of the separated signals. We frame SS as discrete multi-codebook sequence generation, leveraging Encoder-Decoder models to map quantized speech mixtures to target tokens. In addition to the autoregressive modeling strategy, we apply a non-autoregressive model to improve decoding efficiency for residual tokens. Experimental results on LibriMix dataset demonstrate that our approach shows significantly better preservation of linguistic consistency across a variety of downstream tasks compared to existing approaches.
